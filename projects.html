Student-Led Environmental Initiatives

Students are at the forefront of innovation! Here we showcase inspiring projects from schools and universities across the region that are making a tangible difference in environmental sustainability.
Project: The Campus Composting Circle

Goal: To divert food waste from campus cafeterias and use it to enrich soil for a communal urban garden.
Impact: This team successfully reduced the campus's organic waste sent to landfills by 40% and now supplies fresh vegetables to the school kitchen. The project also runs workshops to teach composting to the local community.
Category: Waste Management & Sustainable Food


Project: Sungai Kita (Our River) Monitoring

Goal: To use low-cost DIY sensors to regularly monitor the pH and dissolved oxygen levels of a local river.
Impact: The data collected by the students was used to identify a local factory discharge point. They presented their findings to the Department of Environment (DOE), leading to corrective actions that improved the river's water quality.
Category: Citizen Science & Water Conservation


Project: School Energy Savers

Goal: To conduct a comprehensive energy audit of the school building and recommend efficiency upgrades.
Impact: By recommending switching to LED lighting, setting stricter AC temperature limits, and installing motion sensors, the school was able to reduce its monthly electricity bill by 15%, saving thousands of Ringgit annually and reducing the school's carbon footprint.
Category: Energy Efficiency & Climate Action
