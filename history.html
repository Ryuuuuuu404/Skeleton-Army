History of Earth Day

Earth Day was first celebrated on April 22, 1970 in the United States. It was founded by Senator Gaylord Nelson, who wanted to raise awareness about environmental issues such as pollution, deforestation, and the destruction of natural habitats. More than 20 million Americans participated in the very first Earth Day, making it one of the largest environmental movements in history.

The success of the first celebration inspired countries around the world to become involved in environmental protection. In 1990, Earth Day went global, with over 140 countries joining the movement. This expansion helped boost worldwide cooperation for recycling and environmental conservation.

Today, Earth Day is celebrated in over 190 countries and is recognized as the world’s largest environmental movement. Every year, millions of people take part in tree planting, beach cleanups, climate campaigns, and educational programs to protect our planet.
