Inspiring Voices: Environmental Activists

Every major change starts with an individual voice. This page highlights those who have dedicated their lives to protecting our planet, from global icons to heroes working here in Malaysia.
Rachel Carson (1907-1964)

Pioneer of the Modern Environmental Movement
Rachel Carson was an American marine biologist whose book, Silent Spring (1962), exposed the dangers of indiscriminate pesticide use, particularly DDT. Her work directly led to the establishment of the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) and inspired millions to recognize humanity's connection to nature.


Gurmit Singh

Veteran Malaysian Environmental Advocate
Gurmit Singh has been a key figure in Malaysian environmentalism since the 1970s. As the former head of the Environmental Protection Society of Malaysia (EPSM), he tirelessly campaigned against air and river pollution, deforestation, and irresponsible development. His long commitment sets a strong example for local activism.


The Youth Climate Movement

Driving Global Awareness
Inspired by figures like Greta Thunberg, youth movements worldwide are holding leaders accountable for climate inaction. These young activists use strikes, social media campaigns, and policy advocacy to demand a sustainable future for their generation. Their energy proves that age is not a barrier to change.
